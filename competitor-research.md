## 🏆 Primary Competitor: n8n

**Website:** https://n8n.io  
**Type:** Open-source workflow automation platform  
**Tagline:** "Workflow automation for technical teams"

---

## What n8n Does

n8n is a visual workflow automation tool that lets users connect apps, APIs, and services through a node-based interface. It can be self-hosted for free or used via their cloud service. It's designed for developers and technical teams who want more control than Zapier/Make but less complexity than building from scratch.

**Core Products:**
- n8n Cloud (managed service)
- n8n Community Edition (self-hosted, free, open-source)
- n8n Enterprise (custom pricing)

---

## 💰 Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Price | Features |
|------|-------|---------|
| Self-hosted | Free | Unlimited workflows, unlimited executions (own server) |
| Starter (Cloud) | $24/mo | 2,500 executions, 5 users |
| Pro (Cloud) | $60/mo | 10,000 executions, 10 users |
| Enterprise | Custom | SSO, audit logs, dedicated support |

**Key pricing insight:** Self-hosting is free and only costs the VPS ($5-20/mo), making n8n extremely cost-effective for technical teams.

---

## ✅ Strengths (What They Do Well)

1. **Open-source with full code access** — users can inspect, modify, and self-host everything
2. **Free self-hosting** — unlimited workflows and executions on own infrastructure
3. **Developer-friendly** — supports JavaScript/TypeScript expressions in nodes
4. **400+ integrations** — including custom HTTP request node for anything else
5. **Active community** — large Discord, GitHub presence, regular updates
6. **AI agent built-in** — n8n has native AI agent nodes and LLM integration
7. **Self-host = own data** — appeals to privacy-conscious businesses
8. **Docker deployment** — one-command setup for self-hosted

---

## ❌ Weaknesses (Vulnerabilities to Exploit)

1. **Requires technical knowledge** — node configuration, data mapping, debugging are developer tasks
2. **No pre-built templates library** — users build from scratch; templates marketplace is thin
3. **Steep learning curve** — even for developers, n8n has quirks that take time to learn
4. **No AI-native workflow generation** — you still have to drag and configure nodes manually
5. **Limited support on free tier** — community forums only, no official support
6. **Maintenance burden (self-hosted)** — updates, server management, downtime handling
7. **Debugging complex workflows is painful** — error messages aren't always clear
8. **No built-in LLM fine-tuning** — AI nodes exist but aren't sophisticated for custom use cases

---

## 🎯 Market Positioning

**Target customer:** Developers, technical co-founders, DevOps teams at startups/SMBs who want automation without monthly SaaS fees.

**Sweet spot:** Teams with technical capacity but no budget for expensive Zapier/Make plans.

# Competitor Deep-Dive: Zapier (2026)
**Research Date:** 2026-04-09
**Researcher:** PM Agent

---

## What Zapier Is in 2026

Zapier is the market-leading no-code automation platform connecting 8,000+ apps via "Zaps" (trigger → action workflows). In 2026, it has evolved into a "Unified AI Orchestration Platform" bundling Tables (database), Interfaces (portals), and AI Chatbots alongside core Zaps. It is the default recommendation for non-technical SMBs needing app-to-app automation.

---

## 💰 Pricing (2026)

| Plan | Monthly Price | Tasks Included | Key Limits |
|------|-------------|---------------|------------|
| Free | $0 | 100 tasks/mo | Single-step Zaps only (trigger → 1 action) |
| Professional | $19.99–$29.99 | Starts at 750 tasks | Multi-step Zaps, Filters, Paths, Formatter |
| Team | $69–$103.50 | Starts at 2,000 tasks | Shared app connections, folder permissions, priority support |
| Enterprise | Custom | Unlimited | SSO, SCIM, audit logs, dedicated CSM |

**Additional paid add-ons (2026):**
- **AI Agents add-on:** $33.33/mo → 1,500 "Activity" credits (AI reasoning, web searches, tool calls)
- **Advanced Tables:** +$20/mo (100k+ record limit, custom views)
- **Advanced Interfaces:** +$20/mo (custom branding, custom domains)

**Overage model:** 1.25x multiplier on base task rate when you exceed your tier. (Zaps keep running — no hard cutoff.)

---

## ✅ Strengths (What They Do Well)

1. **8,000+ integrations** — the largest app connection library in the category by far
2. **No-code visual editor** — genuinely accessible for non-technical users
3. **AI Copilot (2026)** — natural language Zap builder: describe a workflow in plain English and it scaffolds it
4. **Zapier Tables + Interfaces bundled** — database and portal builder are included in all paid tiers (a genuine value-add)
5. **AI Agents (2026)** — autonomous agents that can reason across multiple steps, not just simple triggers
6. **Autoreplay** — if an API change breaks your Zap, Zapier auto-retries without you lifting a finger
7. **Filters & Paths are free (2026)** — conditional logic steps don't count toward your task limit, a major cost saver
8. **Massive brand trust** — "Zapier" is synonymous with "automation" for most SMB owners
9. **Shared App Connections on Team plan** — one person connects LinkedIn/Salesforce, the whole team uses it without password sharing

---

## ❌ Weaknesses (Vulnerabilities to Exploit)

### 1. The "Task Trap" — Zapier's Fundamental Problem
This is Zapier's biggest exploitable weakness. **Every successful action in a Zap = 1 task.** More automation = higher monthly bill. As your business grows and automates more, your Zapier bill grows with no ceiling.

- A successful lead sync: 1 task
- An email sent via that same Zap: another task
- A CRM updated: another task
- A Slack message sent: another task

A modest 5-step workflow running 100 times/day = **500 tasks/day = 15,000 tasks/month** — far beyond the 750-task Professional cap.

**The trap:** Success makes Zapier more expensive. When your automation saves you 20 hours/week, your bill jumps.

### 2. Free Plan Is Barely Functional
- Single-step Zaps only (1 trigger + 1 action = useless for real workflows)
- 100 tasks = approximately 10–20 actual automation runs before you're out
- Effectively a perpetual trial, not a real product tier

### 3. Premium Apps Still Count Toward Limits
While some apps (Salesforce, QuickBooks, Shopify) are labelled "premium," they don't add task surcharges — but every *step* in a Zap does. Heavy CRM users burn through tasks fast.

### 4. AI Agents Use a Separate Credit System
Zapier's AI Agents (autonomous, LLM-powered workflows) don't use Tasks — they use **Activities**. Each tool call, web search, or reasoning step = 1 Activity. 400 free/month. $33.33/mo for 1,500 more. This is a second billing dimension most buyers don't anticipate.

- Free plan: 400 activities/month (~48 hours of a "chatty" AI agent)
- Pro add-on: $33.33/mo for 1,500 activities
- Cap: 40 activities per agent run (prevents runaway loops but limits agent capability)

### 5. "Logic-is-Free" Saves Money — But You Have to Know It
Zapier's 2026 clarification that Filters and Paths don't count tasks is a genuine cost-saving insight. But most users don't know this. They pay overage charges because they run every lead through the full Zap, when a Filter could have stopped unqualified leads at zero cost.

**Opportunity:** EveryCompanyClaw's Zapier Templates can be pre-built with optimal Filter/Path placement — saving buyers from Zapier's complexity.

### 6. Advanced Tables & Interfaces Cost Extra
The "bundled" Tables and Interfaces are actually the free/basic versions. Custom branding, custom domains, and >100k records = +$40/mo on top of your base plan.

### 7. Steep Price Jump to Team Plan
Pro ($29.99/mo, 750 tasks) → Team ($69–$103.50/mo, 2,000 tasks). The Team plan is 2.3–3.5x more expensive but only ~2.7x more tasks. For small teams, this jump is painful.

### 8. No One-Time Purchase Option
Zapier is subscription-only, forever. If you automate 1,000 tasks/month on a Pro plan, you pay $19.99/mo indefinitely. EveryCompanyClaw's one-time purchase model is fundamentally more attractive to cost-conscious SMBs.

Don't position EveryCompanyClaw as "replace Zapier." Instead: "Zapier does the easy automations. We give you the Python scripts and prompts to automate everything Zapier can't touch — one one-time price."
