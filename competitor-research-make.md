## What Make.com Does

Make.com (formerly Integromat) is a visual workflow automation platform that lets users connect apps, APIs, and services into automated "Scenarios" via a drag-and-drop canvas. It's positioned between Zapier's simplicity and n8n's technical power — accessible enough for non-coders, powerful enough for complex enterprise workflows.

## Key Features

### Strengths vs. EveryCompanyClaw

1. **Visual Scenario Builder** — Drag-and-drop canvas for building workflows. Beautiful, intuitive UI.
2. **1,200+ Native Integrations** — Huge app ecosystem, far more than most competitors.
3. **Data Manipulation/Transforms** — Built-in JSON, XML, CSV parsing, regex, date/time tools. More powerful than Zapier.
4. **Error Handling & Retry Logic** — Granular controls: breakpoints, retry attempts, fallbacks.
5. **Scheduling & Triggers** — Webhooks, schedulers, polling — all flexible.
6. **Operations-based Pricing** — Pay per "op" not per task (can be cheaper or more expensive depending on workflow).
7. **Make Academy & Templates** — Extensive learning content, template marketplace.
8. **Celonis Integration (Post-acquisition)** — Enterprise gets process mining + automation in one platform.

### Weaknesses / Gaps

1. **No-code but requires learning curve** — More complex than Zapier; not truly "set and forget" for non-technical users.
2. **Monthly operation limits** — Can get expensive fast if workflows run frequently. 1 operation = 1 successful action step.
3. **No built-in AI/LLM features** — AI capabilities are add-ons/integrations, not native. EveryCompanyClaw's AI Prompts Bundle has native AI integration advantage here.
4. **Enterprise-heavy post-Celonis** — Focus shifted toward large enterprise deals; smaller customers may feel neglected.
5. **Limited in-house support** — Community forum-heavy; enterprise support costs extra.
6. **No one-time purchase** — SaaS-only. EveryCompanyClaw's bundle (one-time $49–$149) vs. $9–$299/mo ongoing is a compelling price story.

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Established brand with 10+ years of history
- Robust visual builder with massive integration library
- Celonis backing = enterprise credibility and R&D budget
- Generous free tier drives adoption
- Strong agency/channel partner ecosystem

### Weaknesses
- Subscription fatigue for solo founders and SMBs
- Operation-based pricing confusing and unpredictable
- Post-Celonis pivot toward enterprise alienates indie market
- No native AI features — requires third-party AI integrations
- One-time cost advantage clearly favors EveryCompanyClaw

### Opportunities
- Indie/solo founder market underserved by Make post-Celonis pivot
- One-time payment narrative is a strong differentiator
- AI-native bundles (prompts + scripts) fill a gap Make has
- Content creators/educators looking for automation tutorials = partnership channel

### Threats
- Celonis may accelerate enterprise-only focus, but could also invest heavily in platform
- n8n (open-source) is free and gaining developer mindshare
- Zapier is better-known but more expensive
- Low-code automation space is getting crowded

## What EveryCompanyClaw Can Learn from Make.com

### Copywriting & Positioning
- Make's tagline "Visual Workflow Automation for Everyone" is clear and benefit-driven. EveryCompanyClaw could tighten its own tagline to be similarly scannable.
- Make's comparison page ("Why Make?") is a strong sales asset — consider a comparable "Why EveryCompanyClaw vs. [competitor]" page.

### Product Strategy
- **Make's template marketplace** → EveryCompanyClaw could build a "Template Gallery" or "Automation Recipes" section on the products page, driving both SEO and perceived value.
- **Operations-based pricing confusion** → EveryCompanyClaw's one-time price is simpler to explain. Lean into this: "Pay once, own forever. No surprise bills."
- **Free learning resources** → Make Academy drives adoption. EveryCompanyClaw could create free "Automation Quickstart Guide" as a lead magnet.

### Sales Angle
- **Agency pitch:** Make pitches agencies hard. EveryCompanyClaw could create a dedicated "For Agencies" tier or white-label option.
- **ROI calculator:** Make doesn't prominently feature ROI. EveryCompanyClaw should build a simple "Hours Saved Calculator" on the checkout page — quantify the value: "At $25/hr, this bundle saves you 8h/week = $200/week = pays for itself in [X] days."
